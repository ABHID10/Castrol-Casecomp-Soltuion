How to use this tool

1. Go to Inputs to review or edit demand, plant capacities, production costs, transport rates or hub safety-stock targets — every number is editable.
2. Click Run Plan (top of Inputs tab, or the button below) to re-solve the production & distribution plan against your inputs.
3. Review results in Production Plan, Plant→Hub Flow, Hub→CFA Flow, and Cost Summary. Anything that could not be supplied is flagged clearly in Unmet Demand, with its cost impact — the tool never simply errors out.
4. Inventory Norms shows the redesigned CFA and hub safety-stock / reorder-point / days-of-cover figures feeding this month's requirement, and lets you test different service-level targets live.

What this tool optimises

Objective: minimise Production cost + Plant→Hub transport + Hub→CFA transport + Penalty cost on any unmet CFA demand or unmet hub safety stock.
Constraints respected: plant-line monthly capacity · 25kl production batching · full plant/hub flow balance · single-plant sourcing per SKU (editable rule) · any plant may feed any hub, any hub may feed any CFA.
Priority when capacity is short: the tool protects contractual SKUs and higher fill-rate tiers first, since under-supplying them carries cost well beyond lost margin.

Component 1 — Inventory Norms

Safety stock, reorder point and days of cover per SKU × CFA (957 pairs) and per SKU × Hub (151 pairs), computed from 6 months of sales history, forecast accuracy, and lead-time variability (Exhibits E, G, H). CFA-level targets use the tiered fill rate (A 98% / B 97% / C&D 92%); hub-level uses a flat 98% for all SKUs, per the case brief.

SKU Tiering (A/B/C/D)

All 100 SKUs ranked by 6-month sales volume; cut at cumulative 50% / 80% / 95% of total volume to form tiers A/B/C/D, matching the prescribed 50/30/15/5% volume slabs (Exhibit F).
